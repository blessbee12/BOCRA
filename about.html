Who We Are

Botswana's Converged Communications Regulator

Established under the Communications Regulatory Authority Act 2012, BOCRA is the independent authority overseeing Botswana's entire communications landscape — telecommunications, broadcasting, postal services and the digital economy.

We protect consumers, promote fair competition and ensure that every citizen — from Gaborone's city centre to the most remote village in the Kgalagadi — benefits from reliable, affordable communications.

Our work is guided by the Setswana philosophy of Botho — "a person is a person through other persons." This humanity-first ethos drives every regulation, every consultation, every decision.

Our Mandate

Six Pillars of Oversight

Protecting consumers, promoting fair competition, enabling innovation and ensuring universal access across Botswana's full communications ecosystem.

📡

Telecommunications

Licensing and regulation of mobile, fixed-line and data service providers. Interconnection rates, spectrum allocation and quality-of-service standards across Botswana.

📻

Broadcasting

Oversight of radio and television broadcasters — public, commercial and community. Content standards, licensing, and ensuring diverse Botswana-relevant programming for all citizens.

📦

Postal Services

Regulation of Botswana Post and private couriers. Universal service obligations, quality standards and transformation of postal infrastructure for the digital era.

🌐

Digital Economy

Cybersecurity regulation, digital signatures, e-commerce frameworks and the Universal Access and Service Fund (UASF) driving digital inclusion for all Batswana.

🛡️

Consumer Protection

Championing every communications user's rights. Complaint resolution, quality benchmarks, privacy enforcement and ensuring operators meet their obligations.

📊

Spectrum Management

Managing Botswana's national radio frequency spectrum — licensing, monitoring, interference resolution and efficient allocation for all operators.

Consumer Rights

We Work for Every Motswana

Know your rights as a communications user — and how BOCRA champions them every day.

1

Right to Quality Service

Operators must meet BOCRA's minimum quality-of-service standards. Persistent dropped calls, slow data or poor signal can be reported directly to us.

2

Right to Transparent Billing

You are entitled to clear, accurate billing. Hidden charges and unexplained deductions are violations that BOCRA can penalise operators for.

3

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If your operator fails to resolve your issue within 30 days, bring your complaint to BOCRA. We investigate and act — completely free of charge.

4

Right to Privacy

BOCRA enforces data protection obligations on all licensed operators. Your personal communications data must be handled with security and transparency.

How to File a Complaint with BOCRA

Not satisfied with your operator's response? Escalate to us in three simple steps — we are your last line of defence, at no cost to you.

1
Contact Your Operator First

Give them a fair chance to resolve the issue. Keep records — reference numbers, dates, agent names.

2
Escalate to BOCRA

Unresolved after 30 days? Submit via our website, email, or walk in to our Gaborone offices — no appointment needed.

3
We Investigate & Act

Our team engages the operator and ensures you receive a fair, documented resolution — at absolutely no cost to you.
